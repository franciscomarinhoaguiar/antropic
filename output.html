# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Training Athlete

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete aiming to optimize endurance performance during marathon training:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,500 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (130g), Fat (95g), Carbs (475g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Breakfast (6:30 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with banana (150g), sunflower seeds (25g), and whole milk (240ml). Whole wheat toast (40g) with olive oil (10ml).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 18g, Carbs: 95g

* **Pre-Run Snack (9:00 AM):** Rice cakes (50g) with honey (30g) and seeds (10g).
    * Protein: 3g, Fat: 2g, Carbs: 45g

* **Lunch (12:30 PM):** Lentil and vegetable curry (250g cooked lentils) with brown rice (150g cooked), diced bell peppers (100g), onion (80g), and coconut oil (15ml). Side of cucumber raita with Greek yogurt (100g).
    * Protein: 32g, Fat: 15g, Carbs: 110g

* **Afternoon Snack (3:00 PM):** Smoothie bowl with Greek yogurt (200g), granola (50g), berries (120g), coconut flakes (15g), and banana (100g).
    * Protein: 22g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 85g

* **Pre-Evening Training Snack (5:30 PM):** Bagel (85g) with tahini (20g) and jam (15g).
    * Protein: 10g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 60g

* **Dinner (8:00 PM):** Chickpea and spinach pasta (200g cooked whole wheat pasta, 150g cooked chickpeas) with tomato sauce (150g), zucchini (100g), garlic (5g), and olive oil (20ml). Parmesan cheese (20g).
    * Protein: 35g, Fat: 30g, Carbs: 80g

* **Evening Snack (10:00 PM):** Hummus (40g) with whole grain crackers (30g) and carrot sticks (80g).
    * Protein: 10g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 20g

This meal plan prioritizes complex carbohydrates and plant-based proteins to fuel endurance training, while avoiding all nuts and maintaining vegetarian sources. The timing supports training sessions and recovery needs.
